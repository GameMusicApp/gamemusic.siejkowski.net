Privacy policy for Video Game Music app

Data collection by the Video Game Music app

The Video Game Music app doesn't collect any data on any server nor any where outside your device and your iCloud account. I cannot access, check or process the data in any way.

What data is collected

The app collects the identifiers of the items (albums, artists) that the user has marked as favourites.

How data is collected

The identifiers are stored in a database on the device and synced between devices using the iCloud account via the CloudKit technology. They are never sent nor stored outside the device.

How data is used

The identifiers are used to provide the feature of choosing the favourites to the user and to provide the recommendations based on those favourites. The recomendation algorithm is completely local and on device.

Data collection by the Third Party services

Video Game Music uses Spotify SDK and Apple MusicKit and StoreKit frameworks to allow the user to connect the streaming services accounts and start the playback from the app.

The streaming services are collecting the data about the user listening: when and what music is being started, stoped, paused, skiped.

For details on what Spotify collects, see https://www.spotify.com/us/legal/privacy-policy/

For details on what Apple Music collects, see https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT204881

No other SDKs, analytics tools, advertising tools nor any other similar tools are being used in the Video Game Music app.

How to stop data gathering and how to delete the data

You can stop data gathering by the Spotify and Apple Music by disconnecting their accounts from the Video Game Music app. The option is available in the app settings (gear icon in the right top corner).

You can stop the gathering of identifiers by not adding any favourites. You can delete the already gethered identifiers by removing them from favourites. You can also do it during deleting the app from your device (choose the option to remove the data from iCloud account).
